# DysHarm-Lite-by-3Labr8
a plugin that allows you to create a disharmony effect to your existing audio either via routing to an aux bus or duplicating the audio so each track are on seperate channels. useful for the double wub effect, or other psychoacoustic effects on vocals,instruments and more🔥

March 6th 2026

From 3Labr8 (pronounced elaborate)

To whoever reading this,
Thank you for taking the time to check out my new project that I’ve been working on since 2018.
I first came across disharmony when I was DJing in my home with my teacher at the time. 

He made a joke regarding playing the double bass like the instrument but only instead 
you layer two tracks on top of each other and let the second track only play a specific part of the 
low ends(my preference is ~70hz to ~150hz; I was using Native instruments "Traktor Pro 2" at the time
 so i had to turn down the hi and mid knob then apply 10% of the hi-pass filter and then pitch him up slightly
 so that it creates a series of asymmetry.

Atm in time i wasn't fully aware of the 3 stages of the double wub theory (Bliss,Wavey,Extreme)

 At time I called it the double wub effect from now on I use the technique at private house parties where you know things got crazy and
 when things felt a little crazy, 
I would play a pre-edited recording of a song that’s popular and EDM. It was a herobust song. 	

At the time when I played it things seemed to calm down and the party was able to sober up per se. From then on, I did a little more research into this and can’t really find any info regarding a parallel bass effect so then life went on

 Covid hit. I had to stop DJing because clubs and event companies closed down so then I started to work on hip hop/rap vocal and instrumental  Production. That was 2020 fast-forward to 2025. I go through my old files, there’s a bunch of old music and the pre-edits that I did prior to playing at these house parties way back in the day.

So from then till now I’ve been working on this plug-in at this moment I don’t have an Apple device to configure this plug-in onto logic or ProTools or GarageBand, but I am willing to upload this project on github (i used juce 7.0.12 and visual studio 2017 so that it could be possible for most to make a reiteration of the vst3 plugin i made) for the sake of awareness and to bring to the light that disharmony doesn’t equal bad maybe a little overwhelming at first.
 
 Living in disharmony for a good 20 years maybe affected me in a way that music thats typically played with no disharmony sounds flat. Who knows. I believe that 
once you sit down and actually listen to the difference, 
you hopefully acknowledge that this parallel bass effect 
or the double wub effect creates an imbalance so that way the brain has to process more frequencies that are at play which creates the decompression of the body.

 At the end of the day, music is healing, sound is healing and I’m just waiting for the world to catch up to it but until then.
